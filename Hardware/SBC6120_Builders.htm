Build Your Own SBC6120

All the design files you will need to build your own SBC6120, including the schematics, PC board Gerber images, JEDEC files for programming the three GALs, the HEX files for programming the two EPROMs, and a complete reference manual, the SBC6120 User's Guide, are available at no charge from the Spare Time Gizmos Downloads page.

If you need help obtaining specialized parts such as PC boards, HD6120 CPU chips, programmed GALs or EPROMs, then check the Spare Time Gizmos Orders page for a list of what's currently available.

Spare Time Gizmos cannot promise to provide support for the SBC6120, however if you are a hobbyist building one for personal use and need help, then write to us and we'll do what we can. If your organization would like to put the SBC6120 to use in a commercial application, then Spare Time Gizmos would be happy to enter into a consulting contract with you. Please write to us with your needs.

Resources

The most valuable resource available to SBC6120 builders is our Yahoo! mailing list, where you can meet other people who have built a SBC6120 and many of whom have had the same problems you do! To join the list, just click on the "Join Yahoo! Group" icon at the bottom of this page.

Other SBC6120 resources are

Our list of SBC6120 Parts and Kits for sale.
Our Downloads page, where you can obtain the SBC6120 design files and User's Manual.
Our WinEight emulator, which you can use to develop boot images for the SBC6120.
Datasheets for all the Intersil/Harris 12 bit microprocessor parts including the 6100, 6101, 6102, 6103, 6120 and 6121.
Jim Kearney's bootable OS/8 IDE image in binary for WinEight and in the SBC6120 Download format.
Jim Kearney's archive of OS/8 games in binary for WinEight and in the SBC6120 Download format.
Jim Kearney's instructions on building an OS/8 system for the SBC6120.
The classic Adventure game for the PDP-8 and OS-8.
